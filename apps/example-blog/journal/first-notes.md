# First Notes

Before the archive looked like a journal, it looked more like a fixture: enough to prove sorting, but not enough to show how a post archive feels once you actually start publishing into it.

## Early Decisions

The first draft of the example kept three constraints:

- every post should stay plain Markdown
- the archive should be driven entirely by dates
- the project should remain small enough to inspect quickly

Those constraints are still right. What changed is the quality of the sample content.

## Why The Example Needed Better Posts

Readers understand a blog archive best when the entries have shape:

- a clear opening paragraph
- a couple of subheadings
- enough body copy to make excerpts feel real

Once those pieces are present, the example starts demonstrating structure and tone instead of just mechanics.
