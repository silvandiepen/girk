# Editorial Calendar

One of the first things a small blog needs is a sustainable rhythm. Not a huge content strategy deck, just a repeatable answer to one question: what can we publish consistently without turning the site into a chore?

## The Practical Pattern

For this example, the simplest pattern won:

- one substantial post every few weeks
- short follow-up notes when something changes
- archive pages doing the heavy lifting for navigation

That keeps the writing model lightweight. New posts only need a file, a title, and a date.

## Why It Works Well Here

Folder-driven routing means the archive grows naturally with the content. There is no post registry to maintain, and no additional index file to rewrite whenever a new article appears.

That makes the example useful for documentation teams, solo writers, and product journals alike. If the content already lives in Markdown, the publishing model stays close to the writing model.
