# Reader Feedback

The quickest way to improve a small content site is to watch where readers hesitate. Do they understand what is newest? Can they tell whether a page is part of a series? Do the archive cards give them enough context to choose where to click next?

## Signals Worth Watching

Even in a minimal blog, a few cues matter:

- descriptive post titles
- useful opening paragraphs
- dates that make the timeline obvious

This example does not include analytics, but it is built to support that kind of editorial iteration. The file structure stays straightforward while the archive remains readable as the post count grows.
